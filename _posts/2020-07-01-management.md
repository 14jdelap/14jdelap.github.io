# We need more management training

These are the two most important facts you'll learn today:
1. Management skills can be taught
2. Better management skills significantly increase productivity

The question, then, is: why is there such a low-hanging fruit around?

## Operationalization: what is "management skills"?

- Look into academic research

## To what extent is this being met? What are the chokeholds?

Brainstorm:
- "Management skills" is probably super practical and focused on soft skills, whereas "management education" is theoretical
    - Ie. probably it just isn't being taught
    - It's also less obvious how to teach it
- Human nature
    - We overestimate our management skills (though we think everyone else sucks at it)
    - We're lazy and improving on a skill is hard work — we don't want to do it in our free time
        - Key insight: Masterclass is becoming much more successful than competitors like Udemy because it's **entertainment and inspiration camuflaged as learning**
    - We have an overreliance on credentials as a signal
        - Key insight: Masterclass hacked this by appealing to the best in the field
